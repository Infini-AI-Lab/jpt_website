Jackpot

Optimal Budgeted Rejection Sampling (OBRS) for extreme actor-policy mismatch in RL for LLMs. A practical path to decouple rollout generation from policy optimization without destabilizing learning.

Zhuoming Chen* · Hongyi Liu* · Yang Zhou* · Haizhong Zheng · Beidi Chen Carnegie Mellon University

Abstract (Condensed)

Rollouts dominate the cost of RL for LLMs, yet using a cheaper rollout model creates severe distribution mismatch with the target policy. Jackpot introduces OBRS to move rollout distributions toward the target under a controllable acceptance budget. The framework combines OBRS, a unified training objective that updates both policy and rollout models, and a system implementation that uses top-k probability estimation and batch-level bias correction. Empirically, Jackpot stabilizes training compared to importance-sampling baselines and reaches on-policy performance when training Qwen3-8B-Base for up to 300 update steps.

Why This Matters

Problem

On-policy RL is expensive because rollouts dominate total cost.
Off-policy rollouts introduce large mismatch and unstable training.

Jackpot Solution

Use OBRS to align rollout and target distributions within a budget.
Unified objective keeps policy + rollout models co-evolving.
System optimizations make alignment practical at scale.

Method Snapshot

Optimal Budgeted Rejection Sampling

OBRS directly reduces distribution mismatch by sampling from rollout outputs while enforcing an acceptance budget, ensuring controlled, monotonic alignment.

Unified Training Objective

Policy and rollout models are updated together, mitigating drift and keeping the rollout distribution close to the evolving policy.

Efficient Implementation

Top-k probability estimation plus batch-level bias correction delivers practical throughput without sacrificing alignment quality.
